Title: The Seal of Dartmouth College: A History

Overview: This article delves into the history of the seal of Dartmouth College, exploring its origins, symbolism, and modifications over the years.

Highlights:
- Dartmouth's royal charter mandated the creation of a seal for official documents and diplomas in 1769.
- Founder Eleazar Wheelock designed a seal that resembled that of the Society for the Propagation of the Gospel in order to maintain the illusion that his college was primarily for mission work.
- The seal features an oval circumscribed by a line containing the seal's title, Native Americans, and a label with the words "vox clamantis in deserto."
- The College Publications Committee commissioned a line drawing version of the seal in 1940 by typographer William Addison Dwiggins, which was later modified in 1957 to reflect the college charter's date of 1769 and continues to be used today under trademark number 2305032.
